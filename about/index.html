What is Wholegrain Studios?

Wholegrain Studios is a single developer studio, with an emphasis on wholesome, home-baked games.

It all started when my friend wanted to play a dice game we had seen in Kingdom Come Deliverance (one of, if not the greatest game of all time). We searched around online but nothing really scratched the itch, so I decided to make it myself.

From there, the bakery has grown to include a wide variety of small and large projects that I take pride in thanks to one simple philosophy - I make apps I want to use.

I don't make products to grab people's attention or to earn as much money as possible. I make games that I want to play myself. The user experience is not based on how to optimise play-time, but rather on facilitating the playing of the game.

Who am I?

I'm Jake, a software developer with a passion for authenticity.

I grew up playing games - it's how I bonded with my brothers and even my parents sometimes. Gaming for me has always been a beautiful and expressive art form, in which developers can truly push the boundaries with innovative ideas, wacky art styles, and deeply emotional scenes. However, I've started to notice that games are becoming less and less fun, and maybe it's just because I'm getting old, but maybe it's because there's a missing ingredient.

Games shouldn't be about repeat subscriptions, loot boxes, microtransactions, paid DLCs, extortionate cosmetics, or satisfying shareholders. That, to me, seems a sure-fire way to create a money-grabbing product disguised and marketed as a game. My motivation is creating things I want to play myself. I want to ignite the spark in other people that was ignited in me as a child when I played my first Zelda game.

Whether it's tiny little browser games, or aspirational RPGs, I want to create things that I can truly take pride in. That's my goal, and I hope you enjoy my wholegrain games as much as I enjoyed baking them!
